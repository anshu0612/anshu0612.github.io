Hi, I am

Anshu.

I am an Applied AI Research Engineer based in Singapore, working on synthetic data, multimodal AI, and AI evaluation.

At GovTech Singapore, I build and study AI systems for real-world use cases. I was one of the core contributors behind Mirage, the government's synthetic data generation platform, researching solution gaps and helping design the core engine so it could be more accessible across users and use cases. I also initiated and led DataSharingAssist, an AI-powered tool for data privacy assessment and data-sharing guidance that later inspired this year's Build for Good hackathon.

My recent research explores synthetic video generation and evaluation for safety-critical computer vision tasks, including detecting workers under suspended loads. This work was accepted at the CVPR SynData4CV workshop and extended into an ACM Multimedia Dataset Track submission. I am also exploring research-agent workflows for scientific discovery.

Before GovTech, I was an AI Researcher at the NUS Centre for Research in Privacy Technologies, where I worked on computer vision for human attribute prediction under constrained visual settings, advised by Prof. Mohan Kankanhalli and Dr. Shaojing Fan. I hold a Masters in AI specialisation from the National University of Singapore.

I enjoy collaborating with researchers, engineers, and practitioners to turn applied research into practical AI systems with real-world impact.
