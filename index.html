Hi, I am

Anshu Singh.

I am an Applied AI Research Engineer based in Singapore, working on synthetic data, multimodal AI, and AI evaluation.

At GovTech Singapore, I build and study AI systems for real-world use cases. I led the design and development of the core engine behind Mirage, the government's synthetic data generation platform, and initiated Data Sharing Assist, an AI-powered tool for data privacy assessment and data-sharing guidance. I enjoy working on problems where research ideas need to survive real-world constraints: messy data, user needs, policy requirements, and practical deployment.

My recent research explores synthetic video generation and evaluation for safety-critical computer vision tasks, including detecting workers under suspended loads. This work was accepted at the CVPR SynData4CV workshop and extended into an ACM Multimedia Dataset Track submission. I am also exploring research-agent workflows for scientific discovery, from literature review to hypothesis generation and evidence synthesis.

Before GovTech, I was an AI Researcher at the NUS Centre for Research in Privacy Technologies, where I worked on computer vision for human attribute prediction under constrained visual settings, advised by Prof. Mohan Kankanhalli and Dr. Shaojing Fan. I hold a Master of Computing in Artificial Intelligence from the National University of Singapore.

I enjoy collaborating with researchers, engineers, and practitioners to turn applied research into practical AI systems with real-world impact.
