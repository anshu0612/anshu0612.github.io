Featured work

SynthSite — Synthetic Video Generation for Safety-Critical Vision

A benchmark of rare, safety-critical construction scenarios — synthesized with state-of-the-art video models and evaluated with a structured realism-and-detection protocol.

Safety-analytics models need footage of dangerous events — workers standing under suspended loads — that is unethical and impractical to capture on real sites. I generate these scenarios synthetically with frontier and open video models (e.g. Sora 2 Pro, Veo 3.1, Wan2.2), curate them with vision-language models, and release a labelled benchmark for worker-under-suspended-load detection.

Each clip is scored with a structured evaluation schema that separates generation quality from task difficulty, so the benchmark measures whether a detector actually reasons about the hazard rather than exploiting artifacts:

  • Hazard rubric — visible suspension, worker-in-fall-zone, ≥1s persistence → safe / unsafe.
  • Realism — scene plausibility, geometry, motion, and temporal continuity.
  • Complexity — occlusion, clutter, perspective, small-object and depth ambiguity.
  • QC flags — hallucination, temporal inconsistency, physical implausibility.
